    Night at the Museum: A Scalable Framework for Text-Driven Mesh Motion Generation
    Chenyang Xu, Haoran Li, Zeyu Jiang, Guangzhao He, and 5 more authors
    2026
    Abs Website

    Animating static meshes into realistic motion conditioned on text prompts remains a fundamental yet challenging problem. We propose a semantic-aware and scalable framework for text-driven mesh motion generation. We introduce AniMuse Gaussian Nodes, a novel rigging representation that unifies geometric control with semantic information. The framework requires no manual labelling, making it naturally scalable. A large-scale animal mesh motion dataset is curated to demonstrate scalability.

  2. Under Review
    CRAG: Can 3D Generative Models Help 3D Assembly?
    Zeyu Jiang*, Sihang Li*, Siqi Tan†, Chenyang Xu†, and 7 more authors
    2026
    Abs arXiv

    We reformulate 3D assembly as a joint problem combining assembly and generation. These two processes are mutually reinforcing: assembly provides part-level structural priors for generation, while generation injects holistic shape context that resolves ambiguities in assembly. We design a physically based simulation pipeline to model the fossilization process of bones and implement FractureBEM for modeling brittle fracture behavior.

    GARF: Learning Generalizable 3D Reassembly for Real-World Fractures
    Sihang Li*, Zeyu Jiang*, Grace Chen†, Chenyang Xu†, and 8 more authors
    2025
    Abs arXiv Code Website

    We present GARF, a generalizable flow matching-based 3D reassembly method trained on 1.9 Million fractures, enabling precise real-world fragment pose alignment. Our method generalizes across diverse fracture patterns without category-specific priors.
